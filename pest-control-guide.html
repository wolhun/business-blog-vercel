For many Utah homeowners, spotting a spider is more than an inconvenience—it’s an unsettling reminder that pests are sharing your living space. While some spiders are harmless, others can pose health risks, and nearly all species can cause anxiety. Common Spider Species in Utah

Knowing what you’re dealing with is critical for proper treatment. Utah is home to:

  • Black Widow Spiders – Recognizable by the red hourglass marking, these venomous spiders require immediate attention.

  • Hobo Spiders – Often mistaken for brown recluses, they can deliver painful bites.

  • Wolf Spiders – Large, fast-moving hunters that can be alarming indoors.

  • House Spiders – Common but nuisance-level pests that indicate other insect activity.

Each species has its own habits and preferred hiding places, making identification an essential first step.

Why Spiders Enter Your Property

Spiders typically enter homes and businesses in search of food (other insects) or shelter. Common attractants include:

  • Insects drawn to lights.

  • Warm or sheltered environments during colder months.

  • Cluttered storage areas.

  • Gaps and cracks in doors, windows, and foundations.

Health and Safety Risks

While not all spiders are dangerous, venomous species like the black widow require urgent attention. Even non-venomous bites can cause allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Businesses, especially in food or childcare environments, need immediate removal to maintain health and safety standards.

The Problem with DIY Spider Control

DIY methods—like spraying visible spiders or using sticky traps—may catch a few individuals but rarely address the underlying infestation.

Why DIY fails:

  • Doesn’t address egg sacs that can hatch dozens of new spiders.

  • Fails to remove the food source (other insects).

  • Lacks targeted application to nesting areas.

  • Can scatter spiders into harder-to-reach spaces.
