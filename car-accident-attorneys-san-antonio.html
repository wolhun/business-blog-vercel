The Most Dangerous Intersections in San Antonio

Data collected from the Texas Department of Transportation and San Antonio traffic authorities highlighted these junctions amongst the most perilous in the city:

The Loop 1604 and Interstate 10 Interchange

As one of the busiest highway crossings in San Antonio, especially during congested commuting hours, this intersection frequently witnesses rear-end accidents owing to cumbersome traffic build-up. Improper merging also risks T-bone collisions.

Safety reminder: Stay vigilant when merging lanes and maintain a safe following distance from other vehicles while avoiding distractions.

Highway 281 and Loop 410

This high-speed zone with heavy commercial and daily commuter traffic sees many accidents caused by failures to yield or speeding issues. Its design is also prone to multi-vehicle pileups resulting from abrupt lane changes.

Safety reminder: Slow down on approaching and use turn signals early for lane alterations.

Interstate 35 and Walters Street

Notorious for high-velocity crashes stemming from aggressive drivers attempting to beat traffic lights, it also endangers pedestrians crossing near Fort Sam Houston. Frequent drunk driving wrecks occur here at night and early mornings.

Safety reminder: Exercise caution around speeding vehicles and focus solely on navigating this intersection.

Culebra Road and Ingram Road

As one of the West Side's busiest junctions, its overcrowding regularly leads to rear-end and side-impact collisions. Poor roadwork and insufficient lighting compound crashes here.

Safety reminder: Drive defensively and consistently check for pedestrians and cyclists in this area.

Bandera Road and Loop 1604

A hotspot for high-speed accidents and rear-end pileups, motorcyclists and smaller vehicles face unique peril from heavy truck traffic. Left turns prove especially hazardous because of limited visibility and heavy traffic flows.

Safety reminder: Approach left turns carefully and stay conscious of speeding vehicles.

Why Are Dangerous Intersections so Risky?

Several factors contribute to the consistently high accident rates at these intersections. High traffic brings increased risk, as San Antonio's growing population puts more vehicles on congested roads. Some locations lack clear signage or have deteriorating infrastructure like potholes and poor lighting, complicating safe navigation. Additionally, distracted and aggressive behaviors from some motorists, such as speeding while texting or sudden lane changes, often play a role. The city also struggles with drunk driving, especially on evenings and holidays, leading to more collisions.

If you wind up in a crash at one of these high-hazard hubs, taking prompt action helps protect your health, rights, and financial interests going forward. First, immediately dial 911 to request emergency aid and file an official police report documenting the incident. Be sure to photograph the scene, including any vehicle damage, traffic signals, and injuries. Get contact details, too, from others who witnessed the event unfold.
